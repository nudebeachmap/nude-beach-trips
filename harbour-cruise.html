This is a calm, body-positive cruise for people who enjoy nude beaches — and for curious newcomers who want to try a clothing-optional event in a relaxed setting.

The Experience

Departing from Port Moody, 12pm–4pm, this is a relaxed afternoon cruise into beautiful Indian Arm — with time to swim, float, unwind, and enjoy one of the most scenic stretches of water near Vancouver.

Leaving from Port Moody means less time getting out of the city and more time actually enjoying the setting. We’ll cruise into Indian Arm, settle into the scenery, and anchor in a quiet spot for swim time, with the exact location depending on weather and water conditions.

There’ll be a DJ onboard providing the soundtrack for the afternoon — relaxed, summery, and in tune with the setting. Think good energy, great scenery, and a laid-back atmosphere rather than a full-on party cruise. Once we’re away from busier public areas and conditions allow, the captain will give the go-ahead for clothing-optional time onboard.

FAQ

Do I have to get naked? +

No. Nudity is never enforced.

What matters is intent. If you’re coming because you’re curious and want to try a clothing-optional social space, that’s perfect — even if you’re not sure how fast you’ll jump in.

Some people strip off straight away. Others take their time. Both are totally fine.

Why do you screen guests? +
To keep the vibe relaxed, respectful, and balanced.
Is this a party cruise? +
No. It’s social and fun, but this isn’t a wild booze cruise. Think chill music, good chats, swim time, floaties, and a relaxed crowd.
Is this a dating / lifestyle event? +
No. This is a relaxed, body-positive social cruise hosted by Nude Beach Map — not a dating event and not a “lifestyle” scene.
Are singles welcome? +
Yes — single men and women are welcome. Screening helps us keep a respectful, balanced mix onboard.
What’s the phone / photo policy? +
Consent-first. No photos or video of anyone else without clear permission. If you’re snapping a selfie, make sure nobody’s in the background.
Can I share photos online after? +
Only photos of yourself or your partner with their permission. No posting images where other guests appear without their permission.
Is BYO food and drinks allowed? +
Yes. Bring your own drinks, snacks, and floaties. We want this to feel easy and relaxed — more like a floating nude beach day than a formal hosted charter.
Where do we anchor? +
Somewhere in Indian Arm, depending on weather and water conditions. We’ll aim for a quiet, scenic spot with good privacy and safe swimming conditions.
When does clothing-optional time start? +
Only once the captain gives the OK. We expect that to be once we’re away from busier public areas and conditions allow.
What if the weather is bad? +
Route and anchoring are weather-dependent. If conditions don’t allow for a safe swim stop, we’ll keep it scenic and safe and adjust the plan.
Why is this event 19+? +
Make no mistake — this is a family-friendly vibe. But kids on a moving boat, tricky swim access, and no lifeguards make it necessary to keep this event strictly 19+. It also ensures everyone is of legal drinking age.
