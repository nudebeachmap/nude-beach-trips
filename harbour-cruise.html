A Floating Nude Beach

This is a calm, body-positive cruise for people who like nude beaches and want a rare, clothes-free experience in a comfortable, non-creepy setting.

Not a party boat. Not a dating event. Just summer, scenery, swimming, and a respectful crowd — hosted by Nude Beach Map.

How the day flows

  • 11:00am — Depart Granville Island.
  • We cruise out toward calmer water (route depends on conditions — e.g., Burrard Inlet / Howe Sound / Indian Arm).
  • Once we’re clear of the busy inner harbour and conditions allow, the captain will give the OK for clothing-optional time.
  • We’ll aim for a sheltered bay or beachy spot to anchor with at least 2 hours of swim time (conditions permitting).
  • 5:00pm — Return to Granville Island.

Exact anchoring location depends on weather, privacy, and safety. Captain’s call, always.

Clothing-optional (not mandatory)

Nudity is never forced — but it’s assumed you’re joining for the same reason as everyone else: a rare, clothes-free day that feels easy and normal.

  • Some people strip off straight away. Others ease into it. Both are fine.
  • Respectful behaviour is non-negotiable.
  • This is a non-sexual environment. No “scene” vibes.

The Yacht

You’ll be aboard a custom-designed 94-foot superyacht with plenty of room to spread out, relax, and enjoy the views — without feeling crowded.

Designed for comfort

  • Bow area for front-row views and casual lounging.
  • Sunbeds for peak “do nothing” mode.
  • Flybridge with bar-style setup and unreal BC scenery.
  • Galley with quality appliances for easy onboard food setup.
  • Dining gear (glassware / cutlery) for a nicer vibe than plastic cups.

(In plain English: it’s big, comfortable, and built for a great day out.)

BYO & easy

  • BYO food & drinks (within captain & vessel guidelines).
  • Bring a towel, sunscreen, water bottle, and something warm for later.
  • Swimming is weather/conditions dependent — we plan for it, but safety wins.

Respect policy (the basics)

  • Ask before photos. Give space. Don’t stare. Don’t be a weirdo.
  • Zero tolerance for sexual behaviour, harassment, or pushing boundaries.
  • Hosts and crew may remove anyone who breaks the vibe.

We screen guests to keep the crowd genuine and the atmosphere comfortable.

Phone & camera policy

  • Photos only of yourself or your partner.
  • No other guests in the background — no exceptions.
  • No sharing images of others online without explicit permission.
  • Crew may capture promo content only from guests who opt in.

FAQ

Do I have to be nude? +
No. Clothing is optional. The goal is comfort and respect — not enforcement. That said, the shared intent is a clothes-free experience, so please only apply if that aligns with you.
When is nudity allowed? +
Only after we’ve cleared the busy inner harbour and the captain confirms conditions are appropriate. Captain’s discretion always.
Will we definitely stop for a swim? +
We plan for it and aim for at least 2 hours of swim time, but it depends on weather, sea conditions, and safety. If it’s not safe, we’ll adapt the plan.
Why do you screen guests? +
Screening helps keep the vibe respectful and relaxed — a genuine mix of people who are there for the right reasons. It’s how we avoid awkwardness and keep it feeling like a community.
Is this a party cruise? +
No. This is social and fun, but not a booze cruise, not a “scene,” and not a lifestyle event. Think: chill music, sunshine, swims, and easy conversation.
What’s your cancellation policy? +
Because capacity is limited, tickets are generally non-refundable. If the operator cancels due to safety/operational reasons, you’ll receive a full refund of amounts paid to Nude Beach Trips.
